Research

Research fields and interesting topics.

Energy Economics

My current main research focus is dedicated to electricity market design, particularly focusing on allocation and pricing problems where an increasing share of renewable sources is considered. Important topics here include: investment incentives (game theory); thrutful bidding and expresive bidding languages (auctions); non-convexities in mixed integer linear programs and pricing mechanisms (optimisation and duality theory). My research is related to the Kopernikus-Projekt SynErgie.

e-Commerce & Social Influence

In this line of research I am interested in the problems related to the effects of previous customers into the purchasing decisions of upcoming ones. This is often called Social Influence or Network Effects. The problems I study here are for example assortment, pricing and ranking optimisation using both theoretical approaches (discrete choice modeling, stochastic approximation algorithms and dynamical systems), and data-driven ones.

Machine Learning in Supply Chain Management

The incipient digitalisation is pushing the boundaries of what traditional methods for supply chain methods can do, creating the need for new techniques and technologies that can help managing large scale data. I am currently exploring the use of (deep) reinforcement learning techiques applied to problems such as inventory management and logistics. See for example OR GYM as an attempt to study such type of problems.
